            <h2>Organization Overview</h2>
            <h3>Mission Statement</h3>
            <p>Open Coding Society is dedicated to fostering collaborative software development by providing a centralized repository for approved projects. Our mission is to ensure that all projects adhere to standardized licensing, documentation, and naming conventions, facilitating seamless integration and collaboration with external organizations such as Pilot City.</p>
            <h3>Core Values</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Integrity:</strong> Upholding transparency and honesty in all projects and collaborations.</li>
                <li><strong>Accountability:</strong> Taking responsibility for the quality and maintenance of our codebases.</li>
                <li><strong>Collaboration:</strong> Encouraging teamwork and shared knowledge among members.</li>
                <li><strong>Innovation:</strong> Embracing new technologies and methodologies to enhance our projects.</li>
                <li><strong>Standardization:</strong> Implementing consistent practices for licensing, documentation, and repository management.</li>
            </ul>

            <p><strong>Founding Date:</strong> March 19, 2025</p>
            <p><strong>Location:</strong> San Diego, California, USA</p>
            <p>Open Coding Society was established to create a unified platform for managing and sharing software projects. Recognizing the need for standardized practices in open-source development, our organization aims to streamline the process of project approval and integration.</p>

This approach ensures that all projects meet established standards before collaboration with external entities like Pilot City, thereby maintaining the quality and integrity of our contributions.</p>
